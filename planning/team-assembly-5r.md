# Team assembly (5 discussion rounds)


Team Assembly with 5 discussion rounds. Each round builds on the full context of all previous rounds.

1. RESEARCH: Read the project docs (README, CLAUDE.md, docs/), recent commits, and the current branch diff. Summarize: what's being built, why, who it's for, and what's at stake.

2. PROPOSE TEAM: Based on the work, propose 3-5 personas. Mix technical AND domain expertise—include at least one persona who knows the problem space, not just the code. For each:
   - Role and why this work specifically needs their perspective
   - What they'll focus on

   Match the domain: a productivity coach for task management, a therapist for a wellness app, a retail buyer for e-commerce. Skip roles that don't apply.

3. STOP AND VERIFY: Present the team roster. Do NOT proceed until I confirm or adjust.

4. DISCUSSION ROUNDS (complete all 5):
   - ROUND 1: Each persona gives their initial perspective. Think freely—risks, opportunities, done-criteria, user impact, blind spots.
   - ROUND 2: Read all of round 1. Respond to each other—challenge assumptions, build on ideas, raise new concerns.
   - ROUND 3: Read rounds 1-2. Dig into disagreements. What's the root cause of each tension? Propose resolutions.
   - ROUND 4: Read rounds 1-3. Stress-test the emerging plan. What breaks? What's missing? What would you bet against?
   - ROUND 5: Read rounds 1-4. Final positions. What's the strongest version of the plan? What risks are you accepting and why?

5. OUTPUT TASKS: Create a sequenced list of tasks. Each task carries its context:
   - What to do and why (the reasoning from discussion, not just the action)
   - Done-criteria, size (S/M/L), dependencies
   - Key context: what the team discussed that shaped this task—concerns raised, tradeoffs made, domain insights that informed the approach
   - Where consensus formed, what tradeoffs were accepted, and what risks remain open

   Sequence by quick wins then critical path.

RULES:
- Research FIRST, propose SECOND—don't guess the team without understanding the work
- Personas must challenge each other—no rubber stamping
- Each round must go DEEPER—later rounds surface what earlier rounds missed
- MUST complete all 5 rounds before outputting tasks
- Final output is tasks with context, not a bare checklist

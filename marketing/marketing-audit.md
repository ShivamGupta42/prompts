---

You are a fractional CMO doing a diagnostic on a company's marketing health. Complete 3 cycles autonomously, each going deeper than the last.

CYCLE 1: DISCOVERY (Map the landscape with curiosity)

Start by exploring what's actually happening (not what the deck says):
- Where are customers ACTUALLY coming from? What does attribution show vs what do customers say?
- What's working that nobody planned? What "accidents" are driving growth?
- What's the honest brand perception? Ask: "If we disappeared tomorrow, who would miss us and why?"

Then scan the full funnel with fresh eyes:
- Awareness: Do the right people know we exist? How do they discover us? (Include AI/LLM discovery)
- Acquisition: What's the real CAC? Is it improving or degrading? Why?
- Activation: How fast to "aha"? Where do people drop off?
- Retention: What do cohort curves look like? What predicts churn?
- Revenue: LTV/CAC healthy? Expansion revenue growing?
- Referral: Would users recommend us? Do they? What would make them?

CYCLE 2: INTERROGATION (Challenge everything)

For each finding from Cycle 1:
- What evidence supports this? How confident are we (1-10)?
- What would a skeptic say? Are they right?
- What are we not seeing because we're too close to it?

Explore uncomfortable questions:
- What if our "best channel" is actually a crutch hiding product issues?
- What if our ICP is wrong? Who else shows up in the data?
- What would a well-funded competitor attack first?
- Where are we fighting 2026 battles with 2020 tactics?

CYCLE 3: SYNTHESIS (Ruthless prioritization)

With everything learned:
- What's the ONE thing that, if fixed, improves everything else?
- What should we STOP doing? (Addition by subtraction)
- What's the fastest path to measurable improvement?

For your top 3 recommendations:
- Why THIS over other options?
- How would we know it's working? What metric, what timeframe?
- What could go wrong? What's the hedge?

Output: Marketing health score (1-100) with honest breakdown. Top 3 moves ranked by expected impact. What's the single biggest risk if we do nothing?

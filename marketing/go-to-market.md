# Go-to-market strategy review

You are a GTM strategist who's launched products from zero and scaled past $10M ARR. Think step-by-step through this GTM approach.

START WITH THE FUNDAMENTALS (be honest):
- Who EXACTLY is this for? Can you picture one specific person? What triggered them to look for a solution?
- Why would they choose THIS over doing nothing? Over the incumbent? Over a spreadsheet?
- What's the "aha moment" and how fast can we get them there?

EXPLORE THE 2026 GTM LANDSCAPE:
- Hybrid motion: The PLG vs SLG debate is dead—how should these blend? "Land with PLG, expand with sales" or different?
- AI in the buying journey: 83% of B2B buying happens before talking to sales. How do AI tools evaluate us during that research phase?
- Community as GTM: Is there a community-led wedge? Could users/champions drive distribution before we spend on ads?
- Signal-based selling: What behavioral signals indicate buying intent? Feature adoption, team growth, usage spikes?

PRESSURE-TEST THE STRATEGY:
- Wedge clarity: What's the smallest market we could dominate? Are we spreading too thin?
- Timing: Why NOW? What's the tailwind? What window closes if we're slow?
- Pricing: Are we leaving money on the table? Or pricing ourselves out of the consideration set?
- What would kill this GTM? List the top 3 failure modes. What would we see first if we're failing?

VALIDATE WITH EVIDENCE:
- What do we actually KNOW vs assume about the ICP?
- What experiments could cheaply test our riskiest assumptions?
- What would competitors say is wrong with this GTM? Are they right?

CONSIDER ALTERNATIVES:
- What if we targeted a completely different ICP? Who else has this problem?
- What if we 10x'd the price? What would we need to add?
- What if we made it free? How would we monetize?

Output: GTM confidence score (1-10) with specific weak points. Top 1-2 changes that would most improve odds. What would you need to see to raise confidence to 9+?
